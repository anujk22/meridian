# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Meridian is built for people facing high-stakes education and career choices, and for hackathon judges evaluating whether an AI product produces inspectable reasoning rather than a polished answer. The primary judged user is a graduating computer-science student comparing a stable software role, an early AI startup, and a funded AI research program.

## Product Purpose

Meridian turns a messy decision into a living model. It decomposes the decision into paths, goals, constraints, evidence-backed claims, uncertain factor ranges, and user-owned assumptions. The product succeeds when a silent viewer can see why the recommendation moved and what real-world fact should be verified next.

## Brand Personality

Precise, cinematic, quietly magical. Meridian should feel like a luxury scientific instrument: trustworthy enough for serious reasoning, expressive enough to make model changes memorable, and restrained enough that the decision remains the focus.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a generic purple AI gradient, a SaaS dashboard, a chatbot transcript, a cyberpunk terminal, a childish game interface, or a cluttered analytics product. Avoid decorative motion, stock agent avatars, glass-card soup, and dashboards that present every panel at equal visual weight.

## Design Principles

1. Make causality visible. Every important number change must have a visible reason.
2. Stage the reasoning. Each phase should focus attention on the artifact that matters now.
3. Earn the magic. Motion and depth must communicate decomposition, challenge, or recomputation.
4. Keep values user-owned. Meridian models tradeoffs without pretending to choose the user's priorities.
5. Demonstrate honesty. Ground factual claims, expose uncertainty, and refuse unsupported precision.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA contrast and keyboard behavior. Respect reduced-motion preferences with crossfades and instant state changes. Never rely on color alone for leader, risk, citation, or agent status. Optimize the judged layout for Chrome at 1440×900 and 1440×810 while preventing overflow at narrower desktop sizes.
